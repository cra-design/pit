How to claim

  • Tax year: 2024| Prior tax years: 2022 and earlier
  • Federal tax return: Line 34900
  • Provincial or territorial tax return (if applicable): Line 58969

Steps for claiming

  1. Calculate your federal amount

    • Federal deduction amount

      Choose whichever amount is less:

      • Your calculated total eligible adoption expenses
      • The maximum federal amount for the tax year the adoption period ends

    • Provincial or territorial deduction amounts

      If you are eligible for the federal deduction, choose whichever amount is less:

      • Your calculated total eligible adoption expenses
      • The maximum amount for the province or territory where you resided on December 31 of the tax year the adoption period ends

    If there are 2 adoptive parents, they can split the amount. The total combined claim for eligible expenses for each child cannot be more than the amount before the split.

  2. Claim the deduction on your tax return

    You can only claim adoption expenses in the tax year that the adoption period ends, even if the adoption process took more than one year.

    • Federal tax return

      On line 31300 of your federal tax return, enter the allowable deduction amount.

    • Provincial or territorial tax return

      On line 58330 of your provincial or territorial Form 428, enter the deduction amount if you resided in one of the following provinces or territories:

      • Alberta
      • BC
      • Manitoba
      • Newfoundland and Labrador
      • Ontario
      • Yukon

  3. Keep your supporting documents

    Do not send any supporting documents when you file your tax return. Keep them in case the CRA asks to see them later.
