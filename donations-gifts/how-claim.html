How to claim

  • Tax year: 2023| Prior tax years: 2022 and earlier
  • Federal tax return: Line 31300
  • Provincial or territorial tax return (if applicable): Line 58330

Steps

  1. Calculate your allowable deduction

    Determine which expenses qualify to be claimed. Do not exceed the maximum amount you can claim.

    If parents are spliting the deduction

    Two adoptive parents can split the amount if the total combined claim for eligible expenses for each child is not more than the amount before the split.

  2. Claim the deduction on your tax return

    You can only claim adoption expenses in the tax year that the adoption period ends, even if the adoption took more than one year.

    • Federal tax return

      Claim on line 31300 of your federal tax return

      Anyone who is eligible, can claim the expenses on the federal tax return.

    • Provincial or territorial tax return

      Also, claim on line 58330 of your provincial or territorial Form 428, if, at the end of the year, you reside in:

      • Alberta
      • BC
      • Manitoba
      • Newfoundland and Labrador
      • Ontario
      • Yukon

      Having an income from a source in the above provinces or territory is not enough to qualify. You must be a resident of that province or territory at the end of the tax year you are making your claim.

  3. Keep your supporting documents

    Do not send any supporting documents when you file your tax return. Keep them in case the CRA asks to see them later.
