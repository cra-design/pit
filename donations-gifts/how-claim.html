How to claim

  • Federal tax return: Line 34900
  • Provincial or territorial tax return (if applicable): Line 58969
  • Tax year: 2024| Prior tax years: 2023 and earlier

You may choose to claim all or part of the eligible amount of donations and gifts you made in 2024 on your tax return. If you do not claim the full amount, you may carry forward any unused amounts for up to 5 years (or 10 years for a gift of ecological sensitive land made after February 10, 2014).

There are different limits to the amounts you can claim depending on your situation and the type of donation or gift you made.

In all cases, you may only claim donations that have not already been previously claimed on a tax return.

Steps for claiming the deduction

  1. Calculate your donation and gifts amount

    Include the eligible amount of all donations and gifts either you or your spouse or common-law partner want to claim on your tax return.

    • Federal deduction amount

      Complete Schedule 9, Donations and gifts to calculate your federal donation and gifts amount.

    • Provincial or territorial deduction amounts

      Use the same donation amounts from federal Schedule 9 to complete the donations and gifts section in Part B of Form 428 for your province or territory.

      For all provinces and territories (except British Columbia and Quebec):

        Line 13 from Schedule 9 x lowest rate
      +  Line 14 from Schedule 9 x highest rate
      =  Provincial or territorial donations and gifts amount

      For British Columbia

      There are 3 different rates used to calculate your BC provincial donations and gifts amount. Use Worksheet BC428 to calculate the amount.

      Enter your calculated amount on Form BC428.

      For tax years 2019 and earlier, you may calculate your provincial amount directly on Form BC428.

      For Quebec

      Refer to: Revenu Quebec

  2. Claim the amount on your tax return

    You can claim a tax credit in the tax year you made the donation or in any of the previous 5 years (or previous 10 years for gifts of ecologically sensitive land made after February 10, 2014).

    • Federal tax return

      On line 34900 of your federal tax return, enter the amount calculated on Schedule 9.

    • Provincial or territorial tax return

      On line 58969 of your provincial or territorial Form 428, enter your calculated amount if you resided in one of the following provinces or territories:

      • Alberta
      • BC
      • Manitoba
      • New Brunswick
      • Newfoundland and Labrador
      • Northwest Territories
      • Nova Scotia
      • Nounavut
      • Ontario
      • Prince Edward Island
      • Saskatchewan
      • Yukon

  3. Keep your supporting documents

    The qualified donee must give you a donation receipt indicating the amount you donated.

    If you are filing:

    Electronically

    Keep all your documents in case the CRA asks to see them later.

    By mail

    Attach your completed Schedule 9 to your paper return. Do not send your official donation receipts. Keep these receipts in case the CRA asks to see them later.
